How to Use the Vendo Search Term Analyzer

A quick-start guide for anyone running this tool for the first time.

01
Get Your Report from Amazon

In Amazon Seller Central or Advertising Console:

  1. Go to Campaign Manager → Reports
  2. Select Sponsored Products
  3. Choose report type: Search Term
  4. Set your desired date range (last 30–60 days recommended)
  5. Download as .xlsx
02
Configure Before You Analyze

On the upload screen, fill in:

  • Account / Brand Name — shown in the dashboard header for easy identification
  • Brand Terms to Exclude — comma-separated words that identify your own brand (e.g. stomp rocket, stomp racer). These are filtered out of New Targets so you don't accidentally add your own brand as a keyword.
  • Thresholds — left on AUTO by default, which scales based on account spend size. Flip to MANUAL if you want custom control.
03
New Targets Tab

These are search terms already converting in your auto/PAT campaigns that aren't yet set up as explicit keywords. Adding them as Exact or Phrase match gives you more control and typically lowers ACOS.

  • Strong / Good — add as Exact Match immediately
  • Decent — add as Phrase Match and monitor
  • Weak / Low — keep in auto for now, revisit next cycle

Click the ▶ arrow on any row to see exactly which campaigns and targeting sources triggered that term, and how many orders each generated.

Use ⚙ Edit Filters & Thresholds to update brand terms or min clicks/orders without re-uploading.

04
Negatives Tab

Each row is a search term + campaign combination that spent money with poor results. The key insight: negate at the campaign level, not globally — a term might waste spend in one campaign but convert well in another.

  • NEGATE NOW — $20+ spent with no return. Act immediately.
  • NEGATE — $5–$20 wasted. Worth negating this week.
  • LOW SPEND — Under $5. Monitor, not urgent.

Add negatives as Negative Exact at the campaign level in Amazon Ads. Use the Download CSV button to export the full list for bulk upload.

05
Thresholds Explained

Thresholds control what gets flagged. AUTO mode scales them based on total account spend — a $500/mo account and a $500k/mo account need different bars.

SettingWhat it means
Min Clicks (NT)A term needs at least this many clicks to appear in New Targets. Filters out one-off flukes.
Min Orders (NT)Must have converted at least this many times. Ensures you're targeting proven terms.
Min Spend (Neg)Minimum spend before a zero-sales term gets flagged. Ignores tiny test amounts.
High ACOS % (Neg)Terms above this ACOS with spend are flagged as high ACOS negatives (default 100%).
06
Tips & Best Practices
  • Run monthly — search term data changes fast. Make this part of your monthly optimization routine.
  • Use 30–60 days of data — too short and you get noise, too long and old data dilutes recent performance.
  • Negate before you add — clean out waste first, then add new targets. You'll see ACOS improve faster.
  • Check the dropdown on negatives — a term that looks bad overall may convert well in one campaign. Don't negate it everywhere.
  • All data stays local — this tool runs 100% in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server.
  • Works for any account — just update the brand terms field for each client. No code changes needed.
